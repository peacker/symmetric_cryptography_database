# History of symmetric designs

## Timeline events

1987: FEAL likely the first ARX primitive (block cipher) - https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/3-540-39118-5_24

1997/01/02: NIST announces wish to choose a successor to DES to be known as AES - https://csrc.nist.gov/news/1997/announcing-development-of-fips-for-advanced-encryp
1997/09/12: NIST AES call for proposals - https://csrc.nist.gov/news/1997/requesting-candidate-algorithm-nominations-for-aes
1998/08/20: NIST announced a group of fifteen AES candidate algorithms at the First AES Candidate Conference (AES1).
1998/08: NIST organized AES1 conference
1999/03: NIST organized AES2 conference
1999/04/15: NIST closure of public comment period on the initial review of the algorithms- https://csrc.nist.gov/projects/cryptographic-standards-and-guidelines/archived-crypto-projects/aes-development
1999/08: NIST announces 5 AES finalists - https://csrc.nist.gov/projects/cryptographic-standards-and-guidelines/archived-crypto-projects/aes-development

2007: " Sponge functions" first article defining and analyzing sponge functions - https://keccak.team/files/SpongeFunctions.pdf
2012: first low-latency block cipher PRINCE was proposed at ASIACRYPT
2011/08/11: "Duplexing the Sponge: Single-Pass Authenticated Encryption and Other Applications" first article defining the duplex construction, used for lightweight authenticated encryption - https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-642-28496-0_19
2017/01/01: Farfalle for high-speed authenticated encryption - https://eprint.iacr.org/2016/1188
